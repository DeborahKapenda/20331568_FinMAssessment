Question 2 - Portfolio Construction

Aim of the answer was to illustrate the returns and cumulative returns of the J400 and J200 Index. First a missingness analysis is developed, various plots made to illustrate the fluctuations of the sectors. In particular the Resources division. The calculation of a rolling standard deviation is conducted for the indexes with an example of the Naspers shares.

The data is uploaded and a short missingness analysis is conducted as to visualise the missingness of the data. Seen in the figure below. One notes the 1.37% missing data is found in J400.

Calculating the return of the portfolio can be calculated as \[ r_{\text{portfilio}}(t) = \sum_{i} w_{i}(t) \times r_{i}(t), \] where \(w_{i}(t)\) is the weight of stock \(i\) and \(r_{i}(t)\) is the return of stock \(i\) for all \(i\) stocks.

One conducts the analysis and finds the return for J400 and J200. This provides a histogram plot of the log normal distribution for both indexes.

The period of interest is between 2021-05-23 and 2021-10-29. Plotting the returns for by sector, gives the below plot. As seen from the graph, the resource sector saw a fluctuation in 2020 during the pandemic and has slowly returned to its normal levels the world economy begins to open up, however, still remaining volatile.

A clearer graph is seen below with the sectors as facets - depicting the historical returns for the Top 40 since 2010.

Whose dominating the Top 40 by sector? The figure below shows the Industrial followed by the Financials sector exhibit the highest returns

Given the above visualisation of the data - one has to compare the performance of the different Indexes. To do this, the cumulative return needs to calculated.

First we graph the rolling 120 day Standard Deviation. This figure shows when markets are steady, values of low Standard Deviation are expected.Low Standard Deviation values generally precede major price increases. Analysts usually believe that high volatility is associated with significant peaks, whereas low volatility is associated with significant bottoms.The J200 AND J400 returns seem to be moving in the same direction.

The figures below describe the cumulative returns by sector. High fluctuations is seen the the resource sector, although it is difficult to see the movement of the other two sectors.
